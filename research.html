RuptureNet2D, a nerual network-based surrogate model for rupture dynamics

Earthquake dynamic rupture simulations are crucial for physics-based seismic hazard assessment. However, due to their intricate dynamics spanning vast spatial and temporal scales, these simulations pose a complex and computationally challenging problem. We aim to develop a surrogate model for rupture dynamics, with the ultimate goal of enabling dynamic earthquake inversion.

Quadlayer Krauklis Wave Model for Explaining VLP Tremors

The excitation mechanism behind Very Long Period (VLP) oceanic volcanic tremors is still not well understood. This project aims to develop a rigorous dispersion and oscillation model for a four-layer system that includes an inviscid ocean, a thin crust, a viscous magmatic sill, and an underlying elastic half-space. Such a model could provide valuable insights into the dynamics of VLP tremors.
